There are hundreds of AI repositories and guides out there. Most of them are just noise.
I have deleted 90% of my bookmarks to bring you only what I really use.

If you are trying to go from making basic prompts to building Agents (or fighting with Claude Code), you don't need to have 50 tabs open.

This is my curated list and my real workflow so you don't waste time:

## 1. Step Zero (Anthropic Skills)
Before blindly programming complex skills, I grab the official Anthropic PDF and put it into NotebookLM. I use it to "train" myself and ask technical questions about the architecture before touching a single line of code.

## 2. The Oracle (Claude Code Best Practices)
If you use Claude Code, this repo is mandatory. It is my go-to reference manual whenever I have doubts, the agent gets stuck, or I need to optimize my system prompts.

## 3. The Parts Warehouse (Skills.sh, Antigravity, and Awesome-Agent)
When I have a specific need (an MCP or a specific skill), I don't reinvent the wheel. I search these three directories. It's like having a giant LEGO of already tested functions to get ideas from.

## 4. The Theoretical Basis (Microsoft & AI For Beginners)
And for those days of deep research, where I need to understand the "why" of the models beyond the hype of the moment, I rely on these two gems.

I leave all the links ordered below so you don't have to go crazy looking for them. 👇
